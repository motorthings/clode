### Step 2: Initial Inventory

### Step 3: Content Filtering Strategy

Develop criteria to distinguish authentic voice from reference material by looking for:
- First-person perspective ("I", "we", direct address)
- Stylistic consistency (emojis, formatting, tone)
- Attribution patterns (does it describe others' work?)
- Content type indicators (LinkedIn posts, articles, presentations vs. summaries)

### Step 4: Apply 10-Step Linguistic Analysis

For authentic content samples across the corpus, analyze:

1. **Sentence Structure & Rhythm** - Length patterns, variation, pacing
2. **Vocabulary & Word Choice** - Unique terms, lexical diversity, signature phrases
3. **Tone & Emotional Range** - Mood patterns, emotional modulation
4. **Syntax & Grammar** - Sentence construction, punctuation strategy
5. **Semantic Analysis** - Metaphorical frameworks, conceptual patterns
6. **Pragmatic Analysis** - Audience engagement, direct address, questions
7. **Stylistic Devices** - Rhetorical techniques, repetition, contrast
8. **Narrative Structure** - Temporal framing, perspective shifting
9. **Thematic Consistency** - Core messages, mission alignment
10. **Phonological Features** - Rhythm, emphasis patterns, elliptical constructions

### Step 5: Platform-Specific Analysis

If multiple content formats exist, create comparison table showing:
- Average sentence length by platform
- Emoji/visual element usage
- Question density (per 100 words)
- Hashtag patterns and volume
- We/I ratio variations
- Opening statement patterns

### Step 6: Pattern Identification

Look for evolved strategies not in baseline analysis:
- Visual communication systems (emoji semantics)
- Hashtag architecture (branded, category, engagement tiers)
- Opening statement patterns (aphoristic, inversions)
- Question frameworks ("What if", rhetorical provocations)
- Scientific integration (metrics, citations, studies)
- Temporal layering (past-present-future structure)
- Emotional tone categorization
- Competitive positioning (unique convergences)

### Step 7: Quantitative Metrics

Calculate and report:
- Flesch-Kincaid readability scores
- Unique word percentage
- Sentiment scoring
- Average sentence length by format
- We/I pronoun ratios
- Question density
- Visual element frequency
- Any platform-specific metrics

## Key Principles

**Authenticity Focus**: Only analyze content written BY the author, not ABOUT others or summarizing external sources.

**Evidence-Based**: Every claim about voice patterns should cite specific examples or quantitative metrics.

**Actionable**: Provide templates, checklists, and practical tools—not just descriptive analysis.

**Platform-Aware**: Modern voice adapts across formats while maintaining philosophical consistency. Capture this.

**Quantified Patterns**: Use metrics to validate observations (e.g., "78% of posts open with questions" vs. "often uses questions").
